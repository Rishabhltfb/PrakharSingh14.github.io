Hi!

I am a PhD student with an interest in Solar Physics at Aryabhatta Research Institute of Observational Sciences,Nainital and in joint coordination with IIT Roorkee.

In December 2023, I commenced my PhD journey at ARIES, where I am working with Dr Vaibhav Pant on Exploration of solar coronal jets and associated small-scale flaring processes during solar cycle 24 and 25. We will perform coronal magnetic field modelling in order to understand the magnetic topology and configuration of the region surrounding solar jets.

We will explore the physics of the origin and evolution of solar jets using the high spatio-temporal resolution data from Extreme Ultraviolet Imager (EUI)/Solar Orbiter (SolO), IRIS, AIA/SDO, Multi Application Solar Telescope (MAST), e-Callisto, XSM/Chandrayaan-2 and ADITYA-L1

Prior to this, I pursued postgraduate studies at Banaras Hindu University, specializing in Space Physics. My Master's thesis focused on studying Lower Ionosphere (D layer) properties using Very Low-Frequency Waves under the guidance of Professor Abhay Kumar Singh.

Research

Coronal Plasma Properties using DEM method for various active regions

In 2023, I worked on a project at Manipal Centre of Natural Sciences,Manipal with Dr Sreejith Padinhatteeri. My work there focused on understanding the thermal information of the Sun's optically thin corona using AIA EUV observations. To infer the DEM from AIA data, we applied the regularized inversion technique ( Hannah and Kontar ) to construct the the DEM curve for each binned pixel from six EUV AIA channels. The temperature range chosen for DEM analysis is from 0.5MK to 10MK. Using DEM I calculated the total emission measure as well as the temperature map.

Chromospheric Heating during an Active Region Evolution

During a summer internship @MCNS,Manipal I worked on Solar Heating Problem under the supervision of Dr. Sreejith Padinhatteeri. To explore and understand the link between the structure of the outer atmosphere and its heating, I studied a non flaring active region and observe the its evolution from its growth stage to the decaying phase in different wavelengths using the SDO AIA and HMI instruments. To study the upper photosphere, the transition region and corona of the Sun,we used AIA channels such as 1600 and 171.We also used HMI continuum data to investigate the photospheric characteristics of the active region.

Remote Sensing of Lower Ionosphere using Very Low Freqeuncy Waves.

In my Master Project, I have calculated different ionospheric parameters of lower ionosphere such as electron density, reflection height and propagation distance using the cut-off frequency of different modes of tweeks known as harmonics for three season of 2018 which are summer, winter, equinox using the VLF data received at BHU, Varanasi station. The Automatic Whistler Detector (AWD) helps as to observe and analyse the different VLF waves such as sferics, tweeks, whistlers.
